Metaoverfit: Risk Assessment & Validation

Assesses the risk of spurious findings using the k:p ratio (studies to predictors) and Optimism-Corrected Bootstrap. Prevents "fishing" for significant moderators.

Cross-Validated Heterogeneity (CV-I²)

Standard I² describes inconsistency. CV-I² describes how well a moderator predicts that inconsistency on unseen data (Leave-One-Out).

Conformal Prediction Intervals

Why this matters: Standard prediction intervals assume a normal distribution of effects. Conformal Prediction (Jackknife+/Split) creates intervals with guaranteed coverage (e.g., 95%) regardless of the underlying distribution, making them valid even for skewed or non-normal data.

1. Temporal Synthesis (Recovering Trajectory)

Standard meta-analysis pools a single timepoint (e.g., Week 12). This module pools the entire growth curve (E_max and Rate k), allowing prediction of effects at unmeasured timepoints.

2. Distributional Synthesis (Recovering Shape)

Standard MA assumes normality. This module uses Skewness & Kurtosis to reconstruct the true population shape, identifying responders vs. non-responders.
